The Library

The Al-Qalam University Central Library, presently named Bilya Sanda (Khadimul Islam) Library, was established in 2005 alongside the University. Originally consisting of two major sections, Conventional and Reference, it has since expanded to four departments, sections, and units due to program advancements and improved facilities.

The central library boasts a collection of over 13,000 books and non-book resources. Its e-library section offers access to over 20,000 e-collections via virtual servers, complemented by 24/7 internet services for all university staff and students.

Additionally, college libraries have been established and equipped with resources tailored to their respective programs, enhancing accessibility for students.

Staffing at the library encompasses professionals, para-professionals, and non-professionals, totaling 36 individuals with qualifications ranging from Ph.D.s to library certificate holders, with a substantive University Librarian in the person of Dr. Abubakar Ladan.

Mission, Goals and Strategic Planning...

Our comprehensive plan, coupled with a strategic approach to resource allocation and service delivery, marks a transformative trajectory for the library. Our commitment is to prioritize our users, aiming to position ourselves among the forefront of academic libraries.

This entails not only broadening access to a diverse array of resources but also elevating the quality of our services. We are dedicated to fostering lifelong learning initiatives that establish profound and enduring connections with both our present users and those of the future.

Our library is committed to build and enrich library collections and services to serve the entire Al-Qalam University community and beyond, through a sustained dissemination of knowledge that effectively facilitates the process of teaching, learning, research.
